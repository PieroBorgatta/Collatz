# Phantom Taxonomy Empirical SCC Integration

Date: 2026-05-10

This note closes Phase 7.6 in the empirical sense only.  It integrates
the new `K0 = 16` taxonomy SCC found by the Phase 7.4 run into a
sampled cross-node retention operator and computes finite
Collatz-Wielandt upper expressions on the observed matrices.

## Inputs

- Representative table: `scripts/phantom_taxonomy/phantom_representatives_k3_16.csv`
- Full sampled run: `scripts/phantom_taxonomy/orbit_harness_k16_full_*`
- Doubled-budget stability run: `scripts/phantom_taxonomy/orbit_harness_k16_s16_*`
- SCC reports:
  - `notes/phantom_taxonomy_k16_scc_report.md`
  - `notes/phantom_taxonomy_k16_s16_scc_report.md`

## Result

The `K0 = 16` taxonomy graph has one large observed nontrivial SCC.
At 8 dense lifts/source it has 1222 nodes; at 16 dense lifts/source it
has 1240 nodes.

The empirical substochastic retention operator is subcritical in all
tested views:

| run | view | states | CW upper |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 8 lifts/source | raw node | 1222 | 0.884991533363 |
| 8 lifts/source | `(K,L,b)` | 72 | 0.884858364322 |
| 8 lifts/source | `(K,b)` | 35 | 0.885502907588 |
| 16 lifts/source | raw node | 1240 | 0.885485445971 |
| 16 lifts/source | `(K,L,b)` | 76 | 0.885270267025 |
| 16 lifts/source | `(K,b)` | 37 | 0.885829387560 |

The maximum observed drift between the 8-lift and 16-lift runs is less
than `0.0005`, and the dominant state remains the `K3` / `[1,2]`
family in every view.

## Scope

This is not a theorem-level replacement for the paper's weighted
cross-node certificate.  The transition probabilities are sampled from
the orbit harness, not generated by a deterministic residue-class
certificate.  The result should therefore be cited as:

> empirical integration of the `K0 = 16` taxonomy SCC, with stable
> subcritical observed Collatz-Wielandt bounds around `0.885`.

The next stronger task, if desired, is to replace sampled transition
probabilities by a deterministic/certified transition construction on a
chosen macro-state space, most plausibly `(K,L,b)` or `(K,b)`.
